Who am I? What are my goals? Where did I come from, Cotton Eyed Joe?

Hello. My name is Tyler Clarke, but I respond to "Tyler", "Weird Pusheen", and "dangit". I am a homeschooled author, software engineer, and open source advocate born an' raised here in Georgia. I enjoy literature (British, usually), writing C code, and running my personal website(s). I run Linux full time on everything I can run Linux on (daily driver + server) but my school testing computer, which I occasionally yell at.

My primary goal learning French is just that - I want to become proficient in French, able to speak fluently. Gaining new knowledge is the ultimate meaning of life, according to Plato! I want to be able to talk to my French friends without sounding like a complete idiot, and perhaps be able to scoff jovially and say something derisive about "tourists who don't speak the language" if I ever go to France. Further, being fluent in French will save me a few embarrassing forays into Google Translate and will allow me to write more engaging and accurate French characters in my books, of which I have several.

My parents made sure I knew how to speak some basic words in a few languages - Spanish, French, and German - from an early age. I've also been teaching myself French with Duolingo for a few months (although I have no actual conversational experience, not having any French-speaking friends I see face to face often). Hopefully my outside experience will help me succeed in the class!

Learning a new language is difficult, and I'm not particularly good at learning things outside of my fields - computer science and physics. Hybrid courses can be challenging because you have to keep up with both in-person classwork and an online system.

I plan to complete whatever assignments are due every day before I do anything else, unless I have some particularly pressing tasks to complete for my robotics team. Doing well in this class is definitely a priority for me! D2L BrightSpace's calendar will also help me complete my assignments on time.
